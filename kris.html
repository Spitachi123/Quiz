Vector Operations

Addition of Vector

Two vectors can be added using following laws:

Triangle Law: If two vectors ⃗A and ⃗B are represented by two sides of a triangle in order, their sum ⃗R is represented by the third side in opposite direction.

Mathematically: ⃗R = ⃗A + ⃗B

Parallelogram Law: If two vectors ⃗A and ⃗B have same initial point, complete a parallelogram. The diagonal gives the sum.

Mathematically: ⃗R = ⃗A + ⃗B

Multiplication by Scalar

If a vector ⃗A is multiplied by scalar k, the result is a vector in same or opposite direction depending on sign of k.

Mathematically: ⃗R = k ⃗A

Subtraction of Vector

Subtracting vector ⃗B from ⃗A is same as adding ⃗A and -⃗B.

Mathematically: ⃗A - ⃗B = ⃗A + (-⃗B)

Vector Properties

The commutative property states: ⃗A + ⃗B = ⃗B + ⃗A

The associative property states: ⃗A + (⃗B + ⃗C) = (⃗A + ⃗B) + ⃗C
